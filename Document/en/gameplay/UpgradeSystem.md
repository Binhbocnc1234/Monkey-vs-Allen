# Overview

In Monkey vs Alien, your strength comes not only from tactical decisions in battle, but also from **developing your card collection**. The more you play, the more cards you collect — and the cards you already own can be powered up through multiple levels.

The upgrade system revolves around a single resource: **Shards**.

# Card Ownership

Each card in the game has its own unique shards. When you collect enough shards, you can exchange them for **permanent ownership** of that card. Once owned, the card enters your collection and is available for use in battle.

Cards can also be obtained through other means:
- Basic Monkey is provided from the very first level.
- Winning matches can reward new cards.
- Defeating specific Aliens in Campaign grants the counter card.

# Collecting Shards

After owning a card, you keep collecting its shards. Each card has a **Rarity** tier, and this rarity **affects the drop chance of its shards** — the rarer the card, the harder its shards are to find.

Shards can be earned from:
- Completing Campaign levels.
- Weekly Event rewards.
- Special events.

# Card Upgrades

Once you own a card and have enough shards, you can upgrade it. Each card can be upgraded up to **5 times**, each costing a specific amount of shards.

## Upgrade Types

Each upgrade level grants one of three improvements:

1. **Stat upgrade** — Core stats such as MaxHealth, Armor, MagicResistance, MoveSpeed, AttackSpeed, Strength, etc. are increased by a fixed amount.
2. **New skill** — The card unlocks a brand new active or passive skill, adding new tactical depth.
3. **Skill upgrade** — An existing skill or passive is enhanced — longer duration, higher damage, or additional effects.

## Typical Upgrade Path

Most cards follow this sequence:

| Upgrade | Type | Shard Cost |
|:---:|:---|---:|
| 1 | Stat upgrade | 10 |
| 2 | New skill | 15 |
| 3 | Stat upgrade | 20 |
| 4 | Skill upgrade | 25 |
| 5 | Temporary upgrade | 20 |

The 5th upgrade has a special mechanic: its effect is **temporary**, lasting for a limited time — unlike the first 4 permanent upgrades.

## Examples

**Basic Monkey — Upgrade Path:**

| Level | Effect |
|:---:|---|
| 1 | Strength +5 |
| 2 | New skill — **Monkey Solidarity**: For each Basic Monkey on the battlefield, gain 8% Armor |
| 3 | MaxHealth +40 |
| 4 | **Monkey Solidarity**: Armor gain increased by 4% |
| 5 | Temporary — **Elite Unit**: 10% chance to spawn an Elite unit |

**Basic Alien — Upgrade Path:**

| Level | Effect |
|:---:|---|
| 1 | Strength +5 |
| 2 | New skill — **Advanced**: 15% chance to equip a Cranium Helmet |
| 3 | MaxHealth +40 |
| 4 | **Advanced**: Helmet equip chance increased by 8% |
| 5 | Temporary — **Elite Unit**: 10% chance to spawn an Elite unit |

# Relationships with Other Systems

- **Card System** — Every card can be upgraded, and the card's in-battle effectiveness depends on its current upgrade level.
- **Economy System** — Resource costs in battle remain unchanged across levels, but the Entity's power scales through upgrades.
- **Game Modes** — Different modes offer varying amounts of shards and card acquisition opportunities.
